Alex N

Best LSAT score: 156

I had been working in sports industry for over 10 years before I decided to pursue an MBA. I felt like I had done everything I could in the sphere, and it was the very exhaustion that forced me to look for new opportunities.

Since I was overqualified to apply for a full-time MBA, I took a decision to run for an executive MBA program. I was told that most schools would accept me even with an average eGMAT score, so I was relatively calm waiting for my exam. And then I took a mock test… My score for the math section was terribly low, and the verbal part of the test was, at most, average.

Worrying about the upcoming exam, I rushed to search for some GMAT courses in my native city. Having found none, I purchased a Supreme Prep Pack from Cracker. In less than a month, I had submitted six essays, completed all courses and learnt most of the idioms attached to one of the Sentence Correction topics. Every week I had a session with Kyle, one of the Cracker’s tutors. He gave me important recommendations and built a manageable schedule. In the end of my preparation process I took three diagnostic tests and, pleased with the scores (670, 710, and 660), took the eGMAT test. My score was 156, which, as I later found out, was more than enough. All that being said, thank you guys for getting me prepared in such a short term!

Best GMAT score: 730

I started my GMAT preparation approximately a year ago. Since my final goal was to apply for an MBA into one of the top US business schools, I needed a total score of over 700.

At the very beginning, I thought I could get prepared for the test by myself. I ordered an Official Guide 2016 and spent over two monts practicing. I came home totally devastated and started surfing the Internet trying to understand what had gone wrong. I read a story of one of the guys from India who had failed his first try but hit 690 for his second try in just a couple of weeks, so I decided to take the test again in a month. Unfortunately, my second score was 530.

I spent weeks in search of the most appropriate way to prepare for the test. Eventually, I decided not to disperse my efforts trying to learn everything that was on the Internet.

Having spent over two months on learning with GMAT Cracker Platform, I understood that I had wasted so much time studying on my own. Soon after I started my preparation, my success rate in the verbal section rose by 20%. The platform made me much more confident with the time limit and what I found most useful is the ability to communicate with GMAT tutors right through the platform.

My third attempt exceeded my expectations. Cracker Diagnostic Tests are tough and I scored in the range of 600-710 in all of the diagnostic tests available on the platform. The official GMAT exam, however, made me even more happy: I scored 730!

Finally, I bought a Premium Prep Pack at gmatcracker.com. Speaking of the GMAT Cracker Platform, it is easy to use and has a lot of learning resources that are clear and concise. The most precious thing about the platform is that all that mess of rules is clearly split into topics in such a way that the next topic is somewhat dependent on the previous topic, which gave the feeling of concision and coherence. The platform does not say what I have to memorize but it rather shows how I need to understand this or that rule to learn it more easily. The total time spent on the GMAT preparation reached one year, with approximately 2 months spent working on the platform. My final GMAT score is 720, which I hit for my second try.

Irene F.

Best GMAT score: 680

My success story started when I was in my fourth year of bachelor degree. My parents insisted that I continue my study abroad because the quality of education in my country is far below that in most developed countries, so basically it was my parents decision that I received a masters degree in a foreign country. Since by that time I had been to many countries and was already familiar with many cultures it was not a big deal for me to pick the University. I was choosing between Rotterdam school of management and IE.

later I found out that I had to pass the GMAT exam to apply for either of them. Although the required score was lower than 700, I still worried about my exam because I had never taken any similar test before. After I took CAT, I started worrying even more. The critical reasoning part of the test seemed monstrous to me at that time. My math skills were insufficient to answer correctly at least 60% of the questions in the quantitative section. As a result my first CAT was 410.

I told my parents that I would never score more than 500 for the test. They decided to hire a tutor for me. The tutor helped me greatly with the math section, but sentence correction questions and, especially, critical reasoning tasks were beyond my understanding. I wanted to find a resource which a) contained all the needed material, b) had that material clearly sorted so I had structural understanding of what I was learning, and c) had a sufficient number of questions for me to be able to practice a lot. Eventually, I clicked on the Cracker’s banner on google and bought a premium prep pack.

My first impression was positive. The platform has tons of useful features, among which I would single out a) the test generator, b) a comprehensive list of different GMAT terms and notions, c) clear structure of the test, and, of course, d) online tutor support. The last thing is super helpful. Whenever you are stuck with a question or simply do not understand something, click on a tutor support button and ask a tutor. As a rule, they respond in less than several hours; as I understand, it depends on the time of the day.

My approach towards learning was simple: I first read all the theoretical material and watched video tutorials on a definite topic and only then did practice questions. It is important that the ratio of theory to practice is at least 1:2. I spent up to two months on preparation and then hit 680 on my first try.

One more thing. At the very beginning, I did not understand why I had to take the GMAT exam and it seemed unfair to me that regardless of what major you choose you have to pass this test. However, now I can say that the preparation for the exam was a precious experience and that I learned much from it. This exam sorts out those candidates who are not able to study under pressure, whether it is foreign environment or the necessity to cooperate with people you may not like. Thank you Cracker for giving me an opportunity to study abroad and receive my masters degree at RSM.
